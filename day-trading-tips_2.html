01 — Foundations

Before you place a trade, get these in order

Write a trading plan

Define your setups, entry and exit rules, position size, and max daily loss before the market opens — not while you're in a trade.

Paper trade first

Run your strategy in a simulator until it shows a consistent edge across dozens of trades — before risking real capital on it.

Trade liquid hours

Volume and volatility are highest in the first and last hour of the session. Thin midday liquidity makes fills unpredictable.

Only trade liquid names

Tight bid-ask spreads and high average volume mean you can actually get in and out at the price you expect.

Journal every trade

Log entry, exit, size, reason, and emotion. Patterns in your losses show up in the log long before they show up in your P&L.

Protect the capital first

Your job on any given day is to survive to trade tomorrow. Profit is what happens when risk control is already solid.

Revenge trading after a loss
Step away after a bad trade. Set a cooldown rule — no new entries for a fixed period after a loss.
✕
Moving your stop-loss further away
Once a stop is set, it doesn't move against you. Decide the exit before you enter, not while you're losing.
✕
Oversizing a "high conviction" trade
Conviction isn't certainty. Keep position sizing mechanical — the same rule on every trade, no exceptions.
✕
Trading every setup that appears
More trades usually means more noise, not more edge. Wait for your specific setup and skip the rest.
✕
Using leverage to force a slow account to grow fast
Leverage magnifies losses as fast as gains. Grow size only as consistency proves itself over time.
